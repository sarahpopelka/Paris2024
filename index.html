    Project

    The project aims to equip the IOC and the Paris city government with a technological tool that will provide an exceptional event experience to both Parisians and visitors attending the Paris 2024 Olympic Games. The Paris 2024 app thoughtfully and creatively mobilizes elements of Paris's "city power": its existing app infrastructure (borrowing in concept from the pass sanitaire), the ideology of the Olympic Games (striving toward social and environmental sustainability), Paris's economic positioning (as a major destination for tourism and culture), and bearing in mind the City's legal limitations (including the fact that Paris is bound to the General Data Protection Regulation), in order to strengthen the utility of the application and propmote its by the IOC, the city of Paris, and the multicultural users. The app strives to protect the public, athletes and staff from health and safety risks, while respecting privacy, autonomy, and culutral heterogeneity. This tool provides the public with multiple functions that will facilitate access to events, transportation and mobility in Paris, updates on the Olympics, health recommendations, and security information.

    User Experience

    This application will serve as a one stop shop for attendees and participants to have a smooth and safe Olympics experience. Accessing your tickets will be hassle free, and any information you need, from maps to schedules, will be at your fingertips. The app will also display information about side-events or cultural activities taking place in Paris during the Olympics, thus encouraging residents and visitors to maximize their experience during this fantastic event. In addition to convenience, your health and safety is top of mind. In order to stay vigilant after the COVID-19 pandemic, our in-app chatbot will answer any questions about covid symptoms and getting tested, and you can opt into bluetooth enabled contact tracing for additional peace of mind.

    For your safety, we have integrated a robust security alert and reporting system into the application, you will be able to report any incidents of concern in just a few clicks, and will be immediately notified in the case of an emergency. Furthermore, you can opt-in to share your location with our emergency response teams in a time of crisis.

    Companies that operate under sustainable principles will benefit from the opportunity to highlight their businesses by featuring more prominently in our navigation tool. Users will benefit from supporting these high quality Ile-de-France businesses during their time in Paris.

    Lastly, and in accordance with the "Sport can save lives" message of the IOC and the WHO, the app aims to raise awareness of the importance of physical activity for both physical and mental health, in addition to its benefits for wellbeing and cognitive development. The user can view fitness related messaging at each app login.

    The home screen offers a simple, easy-to-use interface, with all the main functionality up front. The application makes use of large text and a gray color scheme, to ensure accessibility for people with visual impairments.
    The olympics screen allows for quick and easy ticket validation. The user's purchased events show up below the ticket QR code. Clicking into each event shows the full event details.
    The Paris screen integrates with OpenStreetMaps to provide in-app wayfinding and navigation, prioritizing lower-carbon options. In line with the IOC's sustainability tenets, socially and environmentally sustainable businesses are highlighted on the map and in search results.
    The comprehensive help center links users to a variety or health- and safety-related resources. Users can also report a health/safety related problem or place an emergency call from this screen.

    Situational Awareness > Operational Efficiency

    In a world where unpredictable threats can materialize in an instant, the The Paris 2024 app puts realtime data in the hands of decisionmakers, to allow for rapid and efficient reponse when problems arise. The associated Emergency Operations Center (EOC) dashboard comes equipped with realtime intelligence on the attendance of each event and the Olympic Games as a whole (derived from the application), weather forecasting, transportation information (including congestion, transit on-time performance, etc.), public safety calls for service, emergency room capacities and wait times, and the location and nature of the reports/requests made through the Paris2024 app. This data, overlayed with a dense GIS, allows city officials and event organizers to adjust operations on a dime, should any planning need arise.

    We set out to build an app that would provide cutting edge information sharing and emergency response capabilities while still fulfilling user needs and respecting user privacy. This starts with the secure in-app identity verification and ticketing features, which make event attendance at the Games more streamlined and secure. This also guarantees that all attendees will have the app downloaded, which is crucial in case of an emergency. Secondly, the app consolidates FAQs and information resources on anything from Paris transportation to COVID-19 testing in over 100 languages, making it easy to manage and access for organizers and users alike. It also serves as a channel to communicate any updates or health and safety alerts, and health recommendations, associated with sport. Finally, the in-app emergency reporting tool allows for organizers to gather and sort information on situations of concern directly from attendees, who will be prompted to classify the incident by type, severity, and location, facilitating the triage process for the security team, and reporting the most relevant information to the competent authorities. Users may also opt to share their locations in a crisis situation. The app is built with privacy and cybersecurity in mind, and strictly complies with GDPR guidelines.

    Privacy Considerations

    Since inclusivity is a core principle of the app, we designed our terms and conditions as an easy-to-understand frequently asked questions page, so users can fully realize what data sharing structures they are consenting to through their use of the application.

    • Am I required to share any medical information in order to attend the Olympic Games?

      No, you will not be required to submit any medical or health information in order to attend the Olympic Games. Since COVID-19 is now endemic and vaccination rates are sufficiently high, there is not added value to implementing an active disease surveillance mechanism. However, your contact information will be tied to your check-in at each event. If it happens that a concerning COVID-19 variant is linked to an event that you attended, you agree to have public health officials reach out to you as part of a standard contact tracing investigation.

    • Why do I need to have my ID verified on the app? How will that information be used?

      ID verification is used to ensure that the IOC has complete and accurate information on every attendee at the games. This is necessary for two reasons. First, as an accountability mechanism to ensure that no one is inappropriately gaining attendance to the games. Secondly, having complete and accurate information assists responders in the event of a disaster (allowing responders to make sure that every attendee is present and accounted for following an adverse event), and as part of disease surveillance (allowing for contact tracing in the event that a new variant appears). We do not take citizenship or visa status into account, simply identifying information. A copy of your ID will not be stored with your record following verification; instead, a field stating "ID verified" will be attached to your record and visible to IOC/event staff.

    • Who will have access to my data?

      Limited data will be shared with agencies that contribute to the functioning of the Olympic Games, including the IOC, the City of Paris, and public safety agencies. Only data that are relevant to their specific operations, as they relate to the functioning of the games, will be shared, and all identifying information will be omitted. As an exception to this, if you submit a request through the report a problem feature, you can consent to share your contact information with the agency receiving your request, for the purpose of receiving follow-up information. All request types in the report a problem feature will list the receiving agency prior to submission.

    • What happens to my data after the Olympic Games?

      After the olympic games, all personal information will be stored in a secure database for one year, in case it is needed to support contact tracing/ disease surveillance efforts. All other data will be de-identified and utilized in aggregate form, in order to aid in post-event reporting/ metrics, as well as to help improve the operations of future Olympic Games. The data may be made public, so that researchers, planners, and entrepreneurs can benefit from it in their work, but the publicly available data will again be scrubbed of any identifying information and only present relevant variables, in line with current open data standards.

    • Are there any algorithms involved in the processing of my data or as part of the functionality of the app?

      A variety of algorithms are used in the processing of your data and the functioning of the app. For one thing, due to the sheer scale of review that would be necessary, an algorithm is used to verify your identification information. This algorithm uses image processing to pull text-based data from the image that you upload and compare it to the data that you enter at registration. Due to the poor functionality of facial recognition algortihms, particularly with non-white individuals, your ID photo will not be taken into consideration in the ID verification process. For the navigation portion of the application, an algorithm will be used to suggest socially sustainable businesses to you, based on your location and search terms. For the report a problem functionality of the applicaiton, an algorithm will be used to triage your request, based on the details you provide, so that responders are provided with the most pressing issues for review first. The translation services also utilize an algortihm to aid in real-time, rapid translation.

    • Will any biometric information be collected by this app?

      This app does not collect any biometric data, and does not use facial recognition technology in accordance with proposed EU laws and the IOC’s decision against using live facial recognition technology.

    • Will my information be secure?

      Cybersecurity is a top priority for us and for the Olympic games. For this reason, we collect only the strictly necessary information, and store it securely for only a limited amount of time after the Games. In the case of a data breach, all users will be notified expeditiously.

    • Will my contact information be used by the app developer or any other parties?

      You will be required to turn on emergency app notifications from the IOC for the duration of the games, and you may opt in to also be contacted via sms. In the case of COVID exposure, you may be contacted by email or SMS up to a month after the end of the Games. Your contact information will not be shared with any third parties.
